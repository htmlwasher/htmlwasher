Learning Spanish Sites

  • Aprender Español
    Database of world-wide sites that include self-checking activities to practice new knowledge.
  • Cervantes Institute
    The official site for the Cervantes Institute. Here you can find thousands of resources to learn about Spain and Spanish.
  • Cervantes Institute - Aveteca
    includes interactive activities by level.
  • Cervantes Institute - Lecturas paso a paso
    includes level-reading and reading guides.
  • Cervantes Institute - Pasatiempos de Rayuela
    includes Spanish learning games.
  • DELE Ahora
    Includes lots of activities/exercises by level, ranging from A1 to C2; material especially tailored towards the official DELE diplomas; articles on Spanish grammar, vocabulary, music, cinema, Hispanic culture, and more.
  • Lingolia
    Great grammar explanations and self-checking activities to apply your knowledge.
  • Profe de ELE
    A wonderful site including grammar information, interactive self-checking activities, videos, stories, podcasts and much more!
  • Spanish Unicorn
    Stories, audios and grammar activities for an intermediate level.
  • Tandem Language Exchange (APP)
    Use the APP to practice languages with native speakers.
  • TODO-CLARO.COM
    Great cloze tests texts to review grammar and vocabulary.
  • VER-TAAL.COM
    Vocabulary grouped by topic and difficulty with different level activities: recognizing, unscrambling, writing.

YouTube Channels

  • Tio Spanish
    Information on grammar, vocabulary and Spanish customs with funny characters.
  • Tu Escuela de Español
    Great easy and slow-paced grammar information.
  • VideoEle
    Great support channel, illustrating many of the grammar topics with examples from real Spanish streets and situations.
  • SGEL ELE Español para Extranjeros
    Short conversational videos on everyday situations (with subtitles!).

Online Dictionaries

  • Royal Spanish Language Academy
    The Royal Spanish Language Academy offers a free, extremely comprehensive dictionary online. Additionally, if you look up a verb, a ‘conjugar’ link is supplied for you to check all of the tenses - this way you can recognize stem changes, irregular verbs and patterns.
  • Word Reference
    Although there are many translation sites, this one is interesting because it adds many of the use of words in idiomatic expressions and formulaic language. It also includes a forum where specific constructions are addressed.
