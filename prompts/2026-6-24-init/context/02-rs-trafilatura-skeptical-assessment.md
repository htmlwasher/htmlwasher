# Is `rs-trafilatura` Really "The Best" Trafilatura Derivative? A Skeptical Verdict

> Research context document for the htmlwasher TypeScript port.
> Saved 2026-06-24. Source: deep, skeptical research session.

## TL;DR
- **No — not in any independently validated sense.** rs-trafilatura is a genuinely interesting, faster, page-type-aware Rust extractor, but its "#1 / best" claim rests entirely on benchmarks the author built, runs, scores, and maintains himself. On the one *neutral* benchmark (ScrapingHub AEB) its lead over upstream Trafilatura is ~0.006–0.008 F1 — smaller than that benchmark's own ±0.009 bootstrap error, i.e. statistically indistinguishable noise.
- **The headline "F1 0.859 / beats Trafilatura by 6.8 points on dev" comes from the author's own WCXB benchmark**, whose ground truth was generated by LLMs (Claude drafting + four Claude Opus review passes, one human reviewer, no inter-annotator agreement), where the winning tool's per-type profiles were tuned on the same dev set, and whose 7 page-type taxonomy was co-designed with the tool. It is an arXiv preprint (arXiv:2605.21097v1, sole author Murrough Foley), not peer-reviewed, with zero third-party replication.
- **For Contextractor / the TS port: rs-trafilatura is a defensible engineering choice** (Rust-native, napi-rs bindings, no Python runtime, plausibly good on non-article pages) — but it is the *highest-bus-factor, least-validated* option. The lowest-risk "best-proven" extractor remains upstream Python Trafilatura (or, for Rust faithfulness, nchapman/trafilatura-rs / go-trafilatura).

## Key Findings

1. **The ScrapingHub "#1" claim is real but trivial.** rs-trafilatura's self-reported 0.966 F1 on the ScrapingHub Article Extraction Benchmark sits above go-trafilatura (0.960) and Python Trafilatura (0.958) by a margin (0.006–0.008) that is *inside* the benchmark's published bootstrap standard deviation. The official scrapinghub/article-extraction-benchmark README reports trafilatura 0.5.1 at 0.945 ± 0.009 and go_readability at 0.943 ± 0.007 — top tools cluster within a percentage point. On a benchmark of only 181 article pages where every mature tool already scores 0.94–0.96, this is a saturated, table-stakes result.

2. **The big numbers come from a self-built, self-judged benchmark (WCXB).** The "F1 0.859" headline and "+0.068 F1 over Trafilatura on dev" are from the author's own Web Content Extraction Benchmark. On WCXB, Trafilatura is shown at only 0.791 (a 7-type aggregate, dragged down by forums/products) — but on the neutral ScrapingHub benchmark Trafilatura scores 0.945–0.958. The 0.791-vs-0.966 framing is apples-to-oranges.

3. **WCXB's ground truth was made by LLMs, not humans.** Per the paper's own Limitations: initial annotations drafted by Claude, reviewed by four Claude Opus passes, a *single* human reviewer, *no* inter-annotator agreement. The author concedes the LLM-driven stages may introduce systematic biases.

4. **Disclosed but material conflict of interest.** The benchmark author and the winning tool's author are the same person. The paper admits the same dev set was used for both GT quality assurance and extraction-profile tuning. rs-trafilatura's XGBoost classifier targets exactly the 7 page types the benchmark defines.

5. **No peer review, no independent verification.** WCXB is an arXiv preprint (arXiv:2605.21097v1; dataset at Zenodo DOI 10.5281/zenodo.19316874; sole author Murrough Foley). Every reported number traces to the author's own ecosystem (blog, DEV.to, GitHub, a self-run leaderboard at webcontentextraction.org). Numbers are even internally inconsistent across his materials (12 vs 13 vs 14 systems; classifier accuracy 86.6% / 87% / 89%; ScrapingHub 0.966 vs 0.967).

6. **Adoption is minimal; bus factor is extreme.** ~37 GitHub stars, ~11 forks, one author, v0.1.1 first published March 24 2026. Compare upstream Trafilatura: ~6,000 stars, millions of downloads/month, used by HuggingFace, IBM, Microsoft Research, Allen Institute, Stanford.

7. **The ML approach cuts against the strongest evidence in the field.** The SIGIR 2023 study (Bevendorff, Gupta, Kiesel & Stein, doi:10.1145/3539618.3591920) found that heuristic extractors are best and most robust across the board, that large neural models are surprisingly bad especially on complex pages, and — crucially — that "no single extractor performs best on all types of web pages." rs-trafilatura's classifier+profiles approach is a reasonable response to the second point, but adds model-loading, distribution-shift and maintenance risks the heuristic baseline doesn't have.

## Details

### ScrapingHub AEB (the only neutral test)
Official leaderboard: trafilatura 0.5.1 = 0.945 ± 0.009; go_readability = 0.943 ± 0.007. rs-trafilatura's own README: rs-trafilatura 0.966 / go-trafilatura 0.960 / Python trafilatura 0.958. The dataset is only 181 article pages, near-saturated. rs-trafilatura's margin (<=0.008) is within ±0.009 error. The author tested his own build of all three; comparison not run by a neutral party.

### WCXB (the source of every impressive claim)
2,008-page, 7-type benchmark (1,497 dev + 511 held-out): 793 articles, 165 service, 119 products, 117 collections, 113 forums, 99 listings, 91 documentation. Leaderboard: rs-trafilatura 0.859, MinerU-HTML 0.827, Resiliparse 0.797, Trafilatura 0.791. rs-trafilatura "wins" mostly on non-article pages: collections 0.713 vs 0.518, products 0.670 vs 0.562, forums 0.792 vs 0.575. But ground truth is LLM-generated; profiles were tuned on the same dev set; classifier targets the benchmark's exact categories; baseline versions/configs are undocumented. Even within WCXB, on *articles* the tools converge: rs-trafilatura 0.932, MinerU-HTML 0.928, Trafilatura 0.924 — a 0.4-point spread. The "6.8-point win" is almost entirely a non-article-page phenomenon.

### Maturity, adoption, trust
- rs-trafilatura: ~37 stars, ~11 forks, single author, v0.1.1 first published Mar 24 2026, current v0.2.x, MIT/Apache-2.0, ~16K SLoC. No external production users found besides Contextractor.
- Upstream Trafilatura: ~6,000 stars, millions of downloads/month, ACL 2021 paper (doi:10.18653/v1/2021.acl-demo.15), best-in-class on neutral benchmarks. Current v2.1.0 (Jun 7 2026).
- go-trafilatura: ~136 stars, faithful near line-by-line port, near-identical quality.
- nchapman/trafilatura-rs: faithful Rust port with multi-language UniFFI bindings; near-identical quality.

### Technical evaluation
ML page-type classification + per-type profiles is sensible for the genre-dependence the SIGIR paper identifies, but introduces costs upstream avoids: an embedded XGBoost model to load, classification overhead (~44 ms/page total pipeline), brittleness to distribution shift, and ~87% classifier accuracy (≈1 in 8 pages mis-typed). For serverless/Apify, model weight and cold-start matter.

## Recommendations (4 stages)
1. **Fix the claims** (marketing). Stop calling rs-trafilatura's numbers "independent." Separate: (a) "Trafilatura's algorithm wins independent benchmarks"; (b) "our engine adds page-type-aware extraction; on the author's WCXB it leads, but that's self-reported + LLM-annotated."
2. **Validate independently.** Run your own eval on your real page-type distribution with human-labeled ground truth (100–200 pages), comparing rs-trafilatura vs upstream Trafilatura vs nchapman/trafilatura-rs. Threshold: keep rs-trafilatura as default only if it wins by >3–5 F1 on YOUR labeled data.
3. **De-risk the dependency.** Pin the version, vendor the source, control the model file, keep an adapter seam to swap engines. Switch trigger: no commits ~6 months, unpatched correctness/security issue, or your eval failing to reproduce the margins.
4. **Exploit the genuine edge.** If your eval confirms it's better on forums/products/collections, that's the honest differentiator — "page-type-aware extraction validated on our own data."

## Caveats
- Recency: rs-trafilatura and WCXB are ~1–3 months old; "zero independent verification" is partly a recency effect.
- The 0.791 figure is an aggregate, not Trafilatura's article score (0.924 on WCXB articles; 0.945–0.958 on ScrapingHub).
- Conflict of interest is disclosed, not hidden — the problem is structural, not deceptive.
- By code-quality signals (Rust, compile-time regex, dual license, docs) it is a competent project; the issue is unproven superiority, not incompetence.

**Bottom line:** rs-trafilatura may well be the best *Rust* extractor for non-article page types — but as of June 2026 that is an unproven, self-reported claim. "Best on the author's own benchmark" is not "best-validated" or "lowest-risk."
